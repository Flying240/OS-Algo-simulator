Deadlock

Deadlock occurs when two or more processes or threads are unable to proceed because each is waiting for the others to release resources. Essentially, it's a situation where no progress can be made.

Deadlock typically happens under four conditions:

1) Mutual Exclusion: At least one resource must be held in a non-shareable mode. Otherwise, the process would not be forced to wait.

2)Hold and Wait: A process must be holding at least one resource and waiting to acquire additional resources held by other processes.

3)No Preemption: Resources cannot be forcibly taken away from a process; they must be explicitly released by the process holding them.

4)Circular Wait: There are a set of processes {P0, P1, ..., Pn} where P0 is waiting for a resource that is held by P1, P1 is waiting for a resource that is held by P2, ..., Pn is waiting for a resource that is held by P0.

1) Deadlock Detection:

Resource Allocation Graph.

A graph-based algorithm where processes and resources are represented as nodes and edges, respectively, to detect cycles indicating potential deadlocks.

2)Deadlock Aviodance:

Banker's algorithm

It ensurse that all processes resource requests can be granted in a safe sequence without leading to a deadlock.
